HiddenSinger: Singing Voice Synthesis via Latent Diffusion and Self-supervised Speech Representation





Authors



Abstract

Recently, denoising diffusion models have achieved remarkable performances among the generative models in various domains. However, in the speech domain, applying the diffusion model to synthesize time-varying audio has limitations in terms of complexity and controllability, since the audio synthesis requires very high dimensional samples with long-term acoustic features. To reduce the burden of model complexity for singing voice synthesis, we propose HiddenSinger, which can synthesize a high-quality singing voice using a latent diffusion model. To generate high-fidelity audio, we introduce an audio autoencoder, which can encode audio into the compressed audio representation and reconstruct high-quality audio from the quantized latent. Then, we utilize a stochastic differential equations solver to denoise a data-dependent prior noise into the latent representation. Moreover, we extend our proposed model to an unsupervised singing voice synthesis framework to train the model with the unlabeled singing voice dataset. The experimental results exhibit that our model performs better than other models in audio quality. Furthermore, HiddenSinger can synthesize high-quality singing voices with unlabeled data, which trains on only audio.
